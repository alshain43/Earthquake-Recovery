The 1839 Ava Earthquake (Inwa/Amarapura Earthquake)

Earthquake in Inwa, Myanmar

Occurred on March 23, 1839, at approximately 04:00 local time. Epicenter located in the Inwa region in central Myanmar. Estimated moment magnitude ranged from 7.9 to 8.3 magnitude, with some estimates around 7.4~8.0+. The earthquake was caused by rupture along the Sagaing Fault, specifically involving both the Meiktila segment (south) and Sagaing segment (north) of the fault, over a rupture length of roughly 285~325 km, with up to ~5.5m of displacement.

Key Facts About the Earthquake

  • Date: March 23, 1839
  • Time: Approximately 04:00 local time
  • Magnitude: 7.9 - 8.3 (estimated)
  • Epicenter: Inwa region, central Myanmar
  • Fault: Sagaing Fault
  • Rupture Length: 285-325 km

Historical Impact

  • Severe damage to ancient capital of Inwa (Ava)
  • Destruction of many pagodas and religious structures
  • Significant ground deformation and liquefaction
  • One of the largest historical earthquakes in Myanmar
  • Led to changes in settlement patterns in the region
