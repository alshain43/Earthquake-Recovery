1952 Severo-Kurilsk Earthquake (Kamchatka, Russia)

Earthquake in Kamchatka, Russia

Magnitude 8.8~9.0 - the strongest ever recorded in Russia, ranking as the fifth-largest globally. Occurred on November 4, producing a massive 18m tsunami along the Kuril-Kamchatka coast. Devastated Severo-Kurilsk; estimated 2,300-14,000 fatalities.

Key Facts About the Earthquake

  • Date: November 4, 1952
  • Time: 16:58 GMT (04:58 local time November 5)
  • Magnitude: 8.8-9.0 (5th largest recorded)
  • Epicenter: Off coast of Kamchatka Peninsula
  • Depth: 30 km (18.6 miles)
  • Type: Megathrust subduction zone earthquake
  • Fault Length: 700 km (430 miles)

Tsunami Details

  • Generated massive tsunami waves up to 18 meters (59 feet) high
  • Tsunami traveled across the Pacific Ocean
  • Waves reached Hawaii, Alaska, California, and Japan
  • First tsunami to be recorded by tide gauges across the Pacific
  • Provided valuable data for tsunami research and warning systems

Impact and Damage

  • 2,300-14,000 fatalities (estimates vary widely)
  • Severo-Kurilsk completely destroyed by tsunami
  • Town later rebuilt on higher ground
  • Significant damage to fishing industry infrastructure
  • Many villages along Kamchatka coast affected

Aftermath and Legacy

  • Led to improved tsunami monitoring in the Soviet Union
  • Contributed to understanding of Pacific Ring of Fire seismicity
  • One of the first major tsunamis studied by modern methods
  • Highlighted vulnerability of coastal communities to tsunamis
  • Influenced Soviet civil defense and disaster preparedness policies

Geographical Context

The earthquake occurred in the Kuril-Kamchatka Trench, where the Pacific Plate subducts beneath the Okhotsk Plate at a rate of about 8-9 cm per year.
