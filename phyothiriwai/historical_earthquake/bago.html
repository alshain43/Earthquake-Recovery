The 1930 Bago Earthquake

Earthquake in Bago, Myanmar

The earthquake struck on May 5, 1930, early evening local time. It registered a magnitude of approximately 7.3~7.5 magnitude, with most sources supporting 7.4. The quake was triggered by a rupture along the Sagaing Fault, a prominent right-lateral strike-slip fault that runs through central Myanmar.

Key Facts About the Earthquake

  • Date: May 5, 1930
  • Time: Early evening local time
  • Magnitude: 7.3 - 7.5 (estimated)
  • Epicenter: Bago region, Myanmar
  • Fault: Sagaing Fault
  • Fault Type: Right-lateral strike-slip

Impact and Damage

  • Significant damage to buildings and infrastructure in Bago
  • Destruction of historical pagodas and religious structures
  • Ground cracking and liquefaction in affected areas
  • Felt across a wide area of central Myanmar
  • One of the significant earthquakes along the Sagaing Fault
