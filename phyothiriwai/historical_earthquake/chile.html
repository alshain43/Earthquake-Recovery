2010 Maule Earthquake (Chile)

Earthquake in Chile, South America

Occurred on February 27, 2010, at 03:34 local time. Offshore central Chile, approximately 100km north of Concepcion. Registered a moment magnitude of 8.8, a massive subduction zone quake.

Key Facts About the Earthquake

  • Date: February 27, 2010
  • Time: 03:34 local time
  • Magnitude: 8.8 Mw
  • Epicenter: Offshore central Chile, near Maule region
  • Depth: 35 km (22 miles)
  • Type: Megathrust subduction zone earthquake

Impact and Damage

  • 525 fatalities and 25 missing
  • Over 12,000 injured
  • 800,000 people displaced
  • Estimated $30 billion in damage
  • Severe damage to infrastructure, buildings, and roads
  • Power outages affecting 93% of Chilean population

Tsunami Effects

  • Tsunami waves up to 29 meters (95 feet) high
  • Coastal towns severely damaged by tsunami
  • Tsunami traveled across the Pacific Ocean
  • Damage reported in California, Hawaii, Japan, and New Zealand
  • Led to improvements in tsunami warning systems
