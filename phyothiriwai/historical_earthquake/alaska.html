1964 Good Friday, Alaska Earthquake

Earthquake in Alaska, USA

The 1964 Good Friday Earthquake was a magnitude 9.2 megathrust quake in Alaska, the most powerful in US history, killing 131 people, reshaping the landscape, and generating deadly tsunamis that spurred modern tsunami warning systems. Occurred on March 27, 1964 at 5:36 p.m. The quake lasted approximately 4 minutes and 38 seconds, rupturing over 600 miles of fault and releasing nearly 500 years' worth of accumulated stress.

Key Facts About the Earthquake

  • Magnitude: 9.2 (the second strongest ever recorded)
  • Duration: Approximately 4.5 minutes
  • Fault Rupture: Over 600 miles long
  • Vertical Displacement: Up to 38 feet in some areas
  • Tsunami Waves: Reached as high as 220 feet in some locations
  • Aftershocks: Continued for over a year
