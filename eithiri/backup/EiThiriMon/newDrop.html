How to drop during an Earthquake

Step 1

DROP: get low before the quake drops you

As soon as you feel shaking, drop to your hands and knees. This position prevents you from being knocked down and lets you crawl to safety.

Faster is safer
1

Lower yourself quickly

Go down to hands and knees wherever you are. Don't try to run—most injuries happen when people move during shaking.

2

Protect your head & neck

Tuck your chin, keep one forearm over your head as you get low, and be ready to cover fully.

3

Crawl to a safer spot

If you’re near windows, tall shelves, or hanging objects, crawl a few steps toward sturdy cover like a desk.

Wheelchair/limited mobility: Lock wheels, bend forward, and protect your head/neck with arms, a pillow, or book.
