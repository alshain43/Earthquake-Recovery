Objectives


Short-term recovery refers to the immediate actions taken right after the earthquake to save lives and provide urgent assistance.It focuses on medical care,basic needs, and clearing debris to ensure safety.

Short-term Recovery Steps


Emergency Treatment

Providing emergency medical treatment to the injures.

Emergency Response

Supplying clean water, food, and temporary shelter.

Relocating to Safe Zones

Relocating survivors to safe zones is essential to protect them from further danger and provide immediate access to shelter,food, and medical care.

Rapidly Clearing Dangerous Buildings

Rapidly clearing collapsed buildings and debris, as survivors may still be trapped inside.

Removing Death

Removing dead bodies promptly to prevent the spread of dangerous diseases.


Buildings Damage and Crack Assessment in Short-term Recovery


In the short-term recovery phase,assessing structural damage is a critical step ensure public safety.Earthquakes often cause visible cracks in walls,beams, and foundations.Not all cracks indicate the same level of danger;some may be superficial, while others suggest severe structural failure.Therefore,engineers must measure and classify these cracks to determine the next action-whether the building can be repaired, reinforced, or demolished.

Hairline Cracks(less than 1mm)

Usually superficial,caused by plaster damage or surface stress.These can be repaired with minor maintenance.

Moderate Cracks(1mm-5mm)

May indicate partial damage to walls or non-structural elements.Further inspection is required to ensure safety.

Severe Cracks(Over 5mm or diagonal cracks in beams)

These suggest major structural instability.Such buildings are unsafe and must be evacuated immediately.

Professional actions


  • Conduct rapid visual inspection by trained engineers.

  • Use measuring tools such as crack gauges or digital calipers to record crack width and length.

  • Mark and monitor cracks over time to detect further spreading.

  • Categorize buildings into:Safe,Repairable,or Demolition Required.

  • Provide clear signage (e.g.,green=safe,yellow=restricted use,red=unsafe) for public awareness.

Earthquake Recovery-Medium-term Recovery


Medium-term Recovery

Objectives Recovery

Objectives

Medium-term recovery covers the process of rebuilding essential infrastructure and restoring community life within months after the disaster.It aims to bring stability through housing, education, healthcare, and economic activities.

Learn More

Medium-term Recovery Steps

Reconstruction Buildings

Reconstruction house, schools, hospitals, roads, and bridges.

Reviving Economic

Reviving economic activities by supporting jobs and small businesses.

Restoring Government Functions

Restoring government functions means restarting essential services and public safety.It helps people access healthcare, education, and emergency support.This process builds trust and stability in the community.


Medium-term Recovery:Health and Mental Well-being


Physical Health Risks

Minor injuries:Cuts,bruises,fractures.

Infectious diseases:Caused by poor hygiene and contaminated water(e.g.,gastrpintestinal infections, respiratory diseases).

Nutrition and hydration:Limited access to food and drinking water.


Treatment/Prevention


  • Provide first aid using emergency kits.

  • Ensure access to clean water and safe food.

  • Clean and dress wounds properly to prevent infections.

Mental Health Risks


Stress and Anxiety:Feeling unsafe due to loss of home or community.

PTSD(Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder):Severe psychological impact after traumatic experiences such as disasters.

Depression and Sleep Disorders:Trouble sleeping, low mood, loss of motivation.


Treatment



  • Counseling/Therapy:Seek licensed mental health professionals.

  • Community Support:Family, neighbors, and local support groups.

  • Routine and Relaxation:Yoga, meditation, exposure to nature, structured daily activities.
