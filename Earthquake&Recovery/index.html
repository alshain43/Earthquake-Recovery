EARTHQUAKE

You feel the ground suddenly shake, and nearby objects are trembling. An earthquake is happening. They happen millions of times a year, but most are so small people don't even feel them. But powerful earthquakes can cause landslides, tsunamis, flooding, and other dangerous events.

The Earth's crust is broken into tectonic plates that slowly move on top of the semi-fluid mantle. At plate boundaries, stress builds up as the plates collide, pull apart, or slide past each other. Rocks along faults get stuck due to friction, deforming elastically and storing energy like a stretched spring. When the stress becomes too great, the rocks suddenly slip, releasing the stored energy as seismic waves. These waves travel through the Earth, causing the shaking and surface damage we experience as an earthquake.

Earthquake Knowledge

How to Measure Earthquakes Seismic Zones

Measurement

Earthquakes are measured to determine both their strength and impact on people and structures, using two main parameters: magnitude and intensity.

Magnitude

Magnitude is a quantitative measure of the energy released at the earthquake's source. Seismographs record seismic waves. The Moment Magnitude Scale (Mw) is the most accurate scale for all sizes of earthquakes.

Intensity

Intensity describes the observed effects at specific locations, including impacts on people, buildings, and the environment. The Modified Mercalli Intensity (MMI) scale ranges from imperceptible tremors to catastrophic damage.

Importance

Guides structural engineering and safe building design. Helps urban planners identify high-risk areas. Supports emergency preparedness and early warning systems. Provides scientific data for research and disaster mitigation.

Seismic Zones

Plate Boundaries Ring of Fire Intraplate Zones Volcanic Regions

Plate Boundaries — There are three main types of plate boundaries...

  • Convergent: Powerful quakes at subduction and collision zones.
  • Divergent: Moderate events along oceanic ridges.
  • Transform: Shallow quakes along strike-slip faults, like San Andreas.
Key idea: Stress builds at boundaries until sudden slip releases energy as seismic waves.

Global Concentrations — Notable seismic belts include:

  • Pacific Ring of Fire: Most active belt with frequent quakes and volcanoes.
  • Alpine–Himalayan Belt: Stretches from Mediterranean to Himalayas.
  • Intraplate Earthquakes: Occur inside plates on ancient fault lines.
  • Volcanic Areas: Quakes caused by magma movement underground.
Remember: Earthquakes can also occur far from plate edges due to crustal weaknesses.

🌍 Earthquake Safety Guide

Your essential steps to stay safe during and after an earthquake.

During an Earthquake

Drop

Get low on your hands and knees to prevent being knocked over.

Cover

Take shelter under sturdy furniture and protect your head and neck.

Hold On

Hold on to your shelter until the shaking stops.

Stay Calm

Keep calm and follow your safety plan without rushing.

After an Earthquake

Check for Injuries & PTSD

Seek medical attention if injured. Get emotional support to manage trauma.

Turn Off Utilities

Shut off gas, water, and electricity if there is damage.

Stay Away from Hazards

Keep clear of broken glass, debris, and unstable buildings.

Be prepared. Stay safe. Protect your loved ones.

Create an Emergency Plan

  • Identify safe spots in each room.
  • Plan evacuation routes & meeting points.
  • Teach "Drop, Cover, Hold On".

Prepare an Emergency Kit

  • Water & food (3 days supply).
  • First-aid kit, flashlight, batteries.
  • Whistle, mask, documents, small cash.

Strengthen Your Home

  • Secure heavy furniture & appliances.
  • Store fragile items in safe cabinets.
  • Check for structural weaknesses.

Stay Informed

  • Keep a battery-powered radio.
  • Follow official evacuation alerts.
  • Join community safety programs.

Practice Drills

  • Conduct earthquake drills at home.
  • Practice evacuation & meeting points.
  • Include children and elderly.

Insurance & Safety

  • Check earthquake coverage for your home.
  • Keep important documents safe.
  • Review policies annually.

🌍 Earthquake Evacuation Guide

What to do when an earthquake happens

🚨 Immediate Actions During an Earthquake

  • Drop to your hands and knees
  • Cover your head and neck with your arms
  • Hold On to any sturdy furniture until shaking stops
  • Stay away from windows, glass, and exterior walls
  • If in bed, stay there and protect your head with a pillow

If You're Inside

Drop, cover, and hold on under a sturdy table or desk. Stay away from windows, mirrors, and heavy furniture.

Do not use elevators during or after an earthquake.

If You're Outside

Move to an open area, away from buildings, trees, streetlights, and power lines. Protect your head and stay alert for falling objects.

Stay outside until shaking completely stops.

Near the Coast

After the shaking stops, immediately go to higher ground in case of a tsunami. Follow evacuation signs and stay safe.

Do not wait for official warnings - evacuate immediately if near the coast.

If You're in a Vehicle

Stop safely away from bridges, tunnels, and tall buildings. Stay inside the car until the shaking stops. Avoid moving unless necessary.

Set parking brake and avoid overpasses or power lines.

If You're in a Hospital

Stay with patients if you're a healthcare worker. Move patients away from windows and heavy equipment. Protect your head, stay calm, and follow staff instructions.

Know your facility's emergency protocols in advance.

🚨 Emergency Evacuation Guidelines

  • Remain calm and do not panic during an evacuation
  • Follow the designated evacuation routes shown on maps
  • Do not use elevators during emergency evacuations
  • Assemble at designated safe areas away from the building
  • Account for all personnel and report to emergency coordinators

After an Earthquake,Situation at Tsunami and Volcano

Live updates on Volcano, and Tsunami events


Situation at Tsunami


After a strong earthquake,a massive tsunami may strike coastal areas within minutes.


Massive Coastal Flooding

Huge waves rush inland,destroying buildings, infrastructure,and sweeping away people and objects.

High Casualties & Displacement

Many deaths and injuries occur, and large populations are forced into emergency shelters.

Ongoing Hazards

Aftershocks,additional waves,contaminated water,and disease outbreaks threaten survivors.


Situation at Volcano


After a powerful earthquake,nearby volcanoes may become active due to underground pressure.


Increased Eruption Risk

Earthquake activity can push magma closer to the surface,possibly triggering eruption.

Dangerous Hazards

Pyroclastic flows,lava,ashfall, and lahars can cause severe destruction.

Human & Environmental Impact

Forced evacuations, air polution, crop damage, and infrastructure loss.
